Industrialist and philanthropist

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist and philanthropist, best known for his role as the former chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group.

He was born on December 28, 1937.

He played a pivotal role in the acquisition of global brands like Jaguar Land Rover by Tata Motors, which significantly enhanced the group's international footprint.

Ratan Tata is known for his emphasis on ethical business practices and corporate social responsibility

His visionary leadership and commitment to sustainability have left a lasting impact on the Tata Group's operations and reputation.

Ratan Tata's humility, integrity, and philanthropic efforts have earned him respect and admiration worldwide.

He stepped down as Chairman of Tata Sons in 2012 but continues to be an influential figure in the business and philanthropic realms.

His legacy serves as an inspiration to business leaders and individuals aspiring to create a positive impact on society.
